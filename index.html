City Designer's Manifesto

Everbody is a Designer

Cities have the capability of providing for everybody, only because, and only when, they are created by everybody.

Jane Jacobs (1961)

Too often we see designers as experts in possession of some sort of sacred knowledge and magical powers. We need to embrace the fact that design is basic to all human activity – all that we do, almost all the time is design. By embracing the fact that we are all co-creating our environment, we move away from fictional statistical people, treated as interchangeable by city planners, and start to see real people.

All men are designers. All that we do, almost all the time, is design, for design is basic to all human activity. The planning and patterning of any act toward a desired, foreseeable end constitutes the design process. Any attempt to separate design, to make it a thing-by-itself, works counter to the fact that design is the primary underlying matrix of life.

Victor Papanek (1972)
